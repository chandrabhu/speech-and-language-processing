Introduction

Significant developments in the domain of Language Technology started with code cracking efforts dating back to the period of World War II. With the advent of massive online repositories of natural language content, problems of search (or retrieval) and information extraction have rapidly gained interest in the past decades.

Speech and Natural Language Processing are the inter-disciplinary sub-fields of Computational Linguistics, Artificial Intelligence, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering that develop methodologies and technologies enabling the recognition, understanding and translation of spoken languages by computers. Speech Recognition applications include voice user interfaces such as voice dialing (e.g. “Call home”), call routing (e.g. “I would like to make a collect call”), domestic appliance control, search (e.g. find a podcast where particular words were spoken), simple data entry (e.g., entering a credit card number), preparation of structured documents (e.g. a radiology report), speech-to-text processing (e.g., word processors or emails), and aircraft (usually termed Direct Voice Input).

About-Us

Voice recognition refers to identifying the speaker, rather than what they are saying. Recognizing the speaker can simplify the task of translating speech in systems that have been trained on a specific person’s voice or it can be used to authenticate or verify the identity of a speaker as part of a security process.

As speech recognition systems have continued to become more sophisticated and user-friendly more physicians have discovered their merits. According to a Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society report, the use of Speech Recognition Technology (SRT) in the healthcare professions increased from just 21 percent in 2009 to 47 percent in 2013. In addition, a KLAS Research report indicates that 9 out of 10 hospitals plan to expand their deployment of front-end SRT.

The concept of a semantic web is a viable vision; expecting that large-scale semantic structuring of online content will be achieved fully manually-by human authors or editors-is overly optimistic. Web-scale semantic parsing-language technology in pursuit of its ultimate scientific goal, text understanding-will be a prerequisite to realization of the semantic web.

From the technology perspective, speech and language technology have a long history with several waves of major innovations. Most recently, the fields have benefited from advances in deep learning and big data. The advances are evidenced by the worldwide industry adoption of a variety of deep learning methods in designing and deploying speech recognition and natural language systems. These industry players include technology giants like Google, Microsoft, IBM etc.

MACHINE LEARNING AND DATA-DRIVEN APPROACHES

While human language users make highly efficient use of the ambiguity in natural language, this presents a major challenge to computational NLP. A central factor when we try to determine the most likely interpretation of a given utterance is through earlier experiences (eg. in the form of observed frequencies of word combinations and grammatical structures). Language Technology today, models such phenomena in terms of probabilistic models of language structure, mathematically complex systems that ‘learn’ the generalizations over usage patterns from a training distribution.

SaLT Group members are interested in various approaches to such data-driven modeling for a range of NLP tasks, for example dependency and unification-based parsing, natural language generation, dialogue modeling, lexical acquisition, and sentiment analysis.

About SALT Group

SaLT is an interdisciplinary research group consisting of faculties and students from different disciplines of engineering and sciences actively working on problems in the areas of Computational Lingustics and Speech Signal Processing in conjunction with Artificial Intelligence and Robotics.

With the inception of this group, the members aim to create technology that makes it possible for everyone in the world to interact with computers via natural spoken language. Conversational interfaces will enable one to converse with machines in much the same way that humans communicate with one another and will play a fundamental role in facilitating the move toward an information-based society.

The primary motto of the group is to train individuals in the fields of Signal Processing, Robotics and Artificial Intelligence specific to the domain of Language Technology and enable them to be industry ready to solve various unsolved problems in Computational Natural Language for Indian Languages. The group aims to do so by conducting timely workshops, seminars and conferences in association with its advisors from both academia and industry and inturn be up to date with the state-of-art tools, techniques and methodologies

The group also promotes the use and development of various open-source tools in collaboration with individuals and organizations at both national and international levels. It also engages in undertaking various projects from government and private organizations both independently and in consortiums.

The group has an ongoing consortium project sponsored by MeitY, GoI involving 11 other premier institutions of the country titled “Speech-based Access of Agricultural Commodity Prices and Weather Information in 12 Indian Languages/Dialects” and is expected to be delivered by end of July 2017.
